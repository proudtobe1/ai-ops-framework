# Use Case: Incident Review

A structured process for reviewing operational incidents using the AI‑Ops Framework.  
This use case ensures clarity, accountability, and continuous improvement.

---

## 1. Purpose

To provide a consistent, repeatable method for analyzing incidents, identifying root causes, and preventing recurrence.

---

## 2. Components Used

### Workflows  
- `workflow-issue-escalation.md`  
- `workflow-decision-making.md`  

### Templates  
- `template-decision-record.md`

### Systems  
- `system-operating-model.md`  
- `system-decision-model.md`

---

## 3. Trigger Conditions

An incident review is initiated when:

- A blocker impacts delivery  
- A high‑severity risk materializes  
- A cross‑team dependency fails  
- A customer‑impacting issue occurs  
- AI flags a critical risk signal  

---

## 4. Process Overview

1. **Capture the incident**  
   - What happened  
   - When it happened  
   - Who was impacted  

2. **Summarize using AI**  
   - AI generates a structured summary  
   - AI identifies potential root causes  
   - AI highlights related risks or patterns  

3. **Run the Issue Escalation Workflow**  
   - Document severity  
   - Identify owners  
   - Define immediate actions  

4. **Run the Decision‑Making Workflow**  
   - Evaluate alternatives  
   - Select corrective actions  
   - Document rationale  

5. **Create a Decision Record**  
   - Capture the final decision  
   - Assign follow‑up tasks  
   - Define success criteria  

6. **Update Metrics**  
   - Risk exposure  
   - Execution reliability  
   - Velocity impact  

7. **Communicate Outcomes**  
   - Share summary with stakeholders  
   - Update cross‑team dependencies  

---

## 5. AI‑Assisted Steps

AI supports the incident review by:

- Summarizing the incident  
- Detecting patterns or related risks  
- Suggesting corrective actions  
- Highlighting priority drift  
- Generating the decision record  
- Updating metrics  

---

## 6. Output

A complete incident review includes:

- Incident summary  
- Root cause analysis  
- Corrective actions  
- Decision record  
- Follow‑up tasks  
- Updated metrics  
- Communication summary  

---

## 7. Success Criteria

An incident review is considered successful when:

- Root cause is clearly identified  
- Corrective actions are actionable  
- Decision record is complete  
- Metrics reflect the impact  
- Stakeholders are aligned  

---

## 8. Summary

This use case provides a structured, AI‑assisted method for reviewing incidents, ensuring clarity, accountability, and continuous improvement across teams.
