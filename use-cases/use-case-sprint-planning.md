# Use Case: Sprint Planning

A structured, AI‑assisted process for planning upcoming sprint work using the AI‑Ops Framework.  
This use case ensures clarity, alignment, and predictable delivery.

---

## 1. Purpose

To provide a consistent method for selecting, prioritizing, and scoping sprint work while maintaining alignment with organizational goals and operational constraints.

---

---

## 3. Trigger Conditions

Sprint planning is initiated when:

- A new sprint cycle begins  
- Priorities shift  
- A major dependency changes  
- AI detects priority drift  
- Leadership updates strategic goals  

---

## 4. Process Overview

1. **Collect Inputs**  
   - Team updates  
   - Backlog items  
   - Dependencies  
   - Risks  
   - Strategic priorities  

2. **AI Summarization**  
   - AI generates a summary of backlog health  
   - AI identifies risks and dependencies  
   - AI highlights priority drift  

3. **Prioritization Using Decision Model**  
   - Evaluate items using system logic  
   - Score based on impact, urgency, and risk  
   - Identify must‑have vs. optional items  

4. **Capacity & Velocity Check**  
   - Review historical velocity  
   - Assess team availability  
   - AI suggests realistic scope  

5. **Define Sprint Scope**  
   - Select final items  
   - Assign owners  
   - Define acceptance criteria  

6. **Create Decision Record**  
   - Document rationale  
   - Capture alternatives considered  
   - Define success metrics  

7. **Communicate Sprint Plan**  
   - Share summary with stakeholders  
   - Update cross‑team dependencies  

---

## 5. AI‑Assisted Steps

AI supports sprint planning by:

- Summarizing backlog and updates  
- Detecting risks and dependencies  
- Scoring and prioritizing work  
- Suggesting realistic sprint scope  
- Generating the decision record  
- Highlighting priority drift  

---

## 6. Output

A complete sprint plan includes:

- Sprint goals  
- Selected backlog items  
- Owners and acceptance criteria  
- Decision record  
- Risk and dependency summary  
- Expected velocity  
- Communication summary  

---

## 7. Success Criteria

Sprint planning is successful when:

- Scope is realistic and aligned  
- Risks are identified and mitigated  
- Dependencies are clear  
- Decision record is complete  
- Stakeholders are aligned  

---

## 8. Summary

This use case provides a structured, AI‑assisted method for planning sprints, ensuring clarity, alignment, and predictable execution across teams.
