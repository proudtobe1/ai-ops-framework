# System Model: Alignment Engine

A structured model defining how AI agents evaluate, score, and enforce alignment across all work, decisions, and outputs within the AI‑Ops Framework.

---

## 1. Purpose

To ensure AI agents consistently evaluate alignment across:

- Roadmap priorities  
- Team execution  
- Dependencies  
- Risks  
- Decisions  
- Metrics  
- Communication cadence  
- Personas  
- Workflows  

The Alignment Engine is the core logic that prevents priority drift and ensures organizational coherence.

---

## 2. Alignment Dimensions

The Alignment Engine evaluates alignment across six dimensions:

### **A. Strategic Alignment**  
- Roadmap fit  
- Priority match  
- Long‑term value  

### **B. Execution Alignment**  
- Sprint goals  
- Team capacity  
- Delivery timelines  

### **C. Dependency Alignment**  
- Cross‑team coordination  
- Upstream/downstream readiness  
- Dependency health  

### **D. Risk Alignment**  
- Risk posture  
- Mitigation status  
- Escalation needs  

### **E. Metrics Alignment**  
- Impact on operational metrics  
- Trend consistency  
- Metric health  

### **F. Communication Alignment**  
- Cadence compliance  
- Clarity  
- Completeness  

---

## 3. Alignment Scoring Model

AI agents must score alignment using a standardized 0.0–1.0 scale:

- **0.8 – 1.0:** Strong alignment  
- **0.5 – 0.79:** Partial alignment  
- **0.0 – 0.49:** Misalignment  

Scores are calculated using weighted factors from each alignment dimension.

---

## 4. Alignment Evaluation Rules

AI agents must:

- Evaluate alignment for every structured output  
- Identify priority drift  
- Flag misalignment early  
- Recommend corrective actions  
- Update metrics when alignment changes  
- Trigger escalation for severe misalignment  

---

## 5. Priority Drift Detection

Priority drift occurs when:

- Work does not match roadmap priorities  
- Execution diverges from planned goals  
- Dependencies block progress  
- Risks threaten delivery  
- Communication cadence is broken  

AI agents must detect drift and classify severity.

---

## 6. Alignment Correction Protocol

### **Step 1 — Identify Misalignment**  
Determine which alignment dimension is affected.

### **Step 2 — Classify Severity**  
- Low: Minor deviation  
- Medium: Requires adjustment  
- High: Requires escalation  

### **Step 3 — Recommend Corrections**  
Examples:  
- Re‑prioritize work  
- Adjust sprint scope  
- Resolve dependencies  
- Mitigate risks  
- Improve communication cadence  

### **Step 4 — Validate Alignment**  
Re‑score alignment after corrections.

---

## 7. Integration Points

The Alignment Engine integrates with:

- Operating Model  
- Decision Model  
- Metrics Framework  
- Drift Detection Model  
- Failure Modes Protocol  
- Workflows  
- Templates  
- Personas  

AI agents must use the Alignment Engine during:

- Status updates  
- Decision records  
- Risk reviews  
- Alignment checks  
- Escalations  
- Sprint planning  
- Roadmap reviews  

---

## 8. Example Alignment Evaluation

**Input:** Feature X is delayed due to a dependency.  
**Alignment Score:** 0.62 (partial alignment)  
**Issues:**  
- Dependency misalignment  
- Risk misalignment  
- Execution misalignment  

**Recommended Actions:**  
- Trigger dependency escalation  
- Update risk metrics  
- Adjust sprint scope  

---

## 9. Summary

The Alignment Engine ensures that all work, decisions, and AI‑generated outputs remain aligned with roadmap priorities, execution goals, and organizational strategy.  
It is the core mechanism that prevents drift, enforces coherence, and maintains operational integrity.
