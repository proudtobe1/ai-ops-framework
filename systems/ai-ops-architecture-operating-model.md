# AI‑Ops Operating Model

A structured system that defines how AI‑enabled workflows, decisions, and execution layers interact across an organization.

---

## Purpose

To provide a clear, repeatable operating structure that increases clarity, alignment, and execution velocity.

---

## Core Components

### 1. Inputs
- Business context  
- Team updates  
- Data sources  
- External signals  

### 2. AI Processing Layer
- Summarization  
- Classification  
- Prioritization  
- Risk detection  

### 3. Workflow Engine
- Operational workflows  
- Templates  
- Playbooks  
- Communication models  

### 4. Decision Layer
- Prioritization frameworks  
- Risk evaluation  
- Escalation logic  
- Decision trees  

### 5. Execution Layer
- Operating cadence  
- Reporting rhythm  
- Automation  
- Cross‑functional alignment  

---

## Operating Principles

### Clarity
Every workflow and decision must produce a clear, unambiguous output.

### Alignment
Teams operate from shared context and shared priorities.

### Velocity
AI accelerates decision‑making and reduces operational drag.

### Repeatability
Processes are documented, modular, and reusable.

---

## AI‑Assisted Components
- Automated summaries  
- Priority recommendations  
- Risk surfacing  
- Communication generation  
- Decision support
