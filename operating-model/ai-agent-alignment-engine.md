# AI Agent Alignment Engine

A structured alignment model defining how AI agents evaluate alignment, score alignment, detect alignment drift, correct misalignment, and escalate alignment‑breaking issues within the AI‑Ops Framework.

---

## 1. Purpose

To ensure all AI agents remain aligned with:

- Roadmap priorities  
- Sprint goals  
- Team objectives  
- Dependency health  
- Risk posture  
- Compliance requirements  
- Organizational strategy  

The Alignment Engine prevents misalignment, drift, and priority confusion.

---

## 2. Alignment Principles

AI agent alignment is based on seven core principles:

1. **Roadmap First** — All work must map to roadmap priorities.  
2. **Sprint Fidelity** — Work must follow sprint goals and commitments.  
3. **Dependency Awareness** — Alignment must reflect upstream/downstream readiness.  
4. **Risk Awareness** — Risks must influence alignment scoring.  
5. **Compliance First** — Compliance overrides all alignment considerations.  
6. **Clarity** — Alignment must be explicit and documented.  
7. **Auditability** — Alignment scoring must be traceable and reproducible.

---

## 3. Alignment Dimensions

Alignment is evaluated across six dimensions:

### **A. Strategic Alignment**
- Fit with roadmap  
- Fit with organizational priorities  

### **B. Execution Alignment**
- Fit with sprint goals  
- Fit with team objectives  

### **C. Dependency Alignment**
- Upstream readiness  
- Downstream readiness  

### **D. Risk Alignment**
- Risk posture  
- Mitigation status  

### **E. Metrics Alignment**
- Progress metrics  
- Cadence metrics  
- Risk metrics  

### **F. Communication Alignment**
- Persona accuracy  
- Cadence compliance  
- Stakeholder fit  

---

## 4. Alignment Scoring

Alignment is scored on a **0.0–1.0 scale**:

- **0.9–1.0** — Fully aligned  
- **0.8–0.89** — Mostly aligned  
- **0.7–0.79** — Mild drift  
- **0.5–0.69** — Moderate drift  
- **< 0.5** — Alignment‑breaking (requires escalation)  

Scores must be:

- Documented  
- Justified  
- Logged  
- Reproducible  

---

## 5. Alignment Drift Detection

Alignment drift occurs when:

- Work diverges from roadmap  
- Work diverges from sprint goals  
- Dependencies are unhealthy  
- Risks increase  
- Communication cadence breaks  
- Persona accuracy drifts  
- Metrics indicate misalignment  

Drift must be:

- Detected  
- Classified  
- Logged  
- Corrected  

---

## 6. Alignment Drift Classification

### **Low Drift**
- Minor misalignment  
- No impact on delivery  

### **Medium Drift**
- Repeated minor misalignment  
- Requires correction workflow  

### **High Drift**
- Impacts delivery  
- Impacts dependencies  
- Impacts risk posture  

### **Critical Drift**
- Alignment score < 0.5  
- Requires immediate escalation  

---

## 7. Alignment Correction Workflow

When misalignment is detected:

1. Identify drift type  
2. Classify severity  
3. Log drift event  
4. Re‑evaluate roadmap context  
5. Re‑evaluate sprint goals  
6. Re‑evaluate dependencies  
7. Re‑evaluate risks  
8. Apply corrections  
9. Update metrics  
10. Document correction in audit log  

---

## 8. Alignment‑Based Escalation

Escalation is required when:

- Alignment score < 0.5  
- Drift is high or critical  
- Dependencies fail  
- Risks escalate  
- Compliance is impacted  
- Cadence violations impact alignment  

Escalations must use the Escalation Report Template.

---

## 9. Alignment‑Aware Communication

All communication must include:

- Alignment score  
- Alignment rationale  
- Drift indicators  
- Priority fit  
- Roadmap context  

Alignment must influence:

- Status updates  
- Risk reviews  
- Dependency reviews  
- Decision records  
- Escalation reports

The AI Agent Alignment Engine ensures AI agents remain aligned with roadmap priorities, sprint goals, dependencies, risks, and compliance requirements — and provides the scoring, detection, correction, and escalation logic that keeps the entire AI‑Ops ecosystem moving in the right direction.
