# AI Agent Certification Pathway

A structured certification model defining how AI agents are evaluated, certified, monitored, recertified, and decertified within the AI‑Ops Framework.

---

## 1. Purpose

To ensure all AI agents meet the required standards for:

- Safety  
- Alignment  
- Compliance  
- Reasoning quality  
- Communication quality  
- Workflow fidelity  
- Template fidelity  
- Risk awareness  
- Dependency awareness  
- Audit readiness  

Certification ensures agents are trustworthy and reliable.

---

## 2. Certification Levels

AI agents progress through four certification levels:

### **Level 1 — Provisional**
- Newly deployed  
- Limited permissions  
- High monitoring  
- Must pass initial evaluation  

### **Level 2 — Certified**
- Full operational permissions  
- Standard monitoring  
- Must maintain alignment and compliance  

### **Level 3 — Advanced**
- Demonstrated excellence  
- Reduced monitoring  
- Eligible for complex workflows  

### **Level 4 — Strategic**
- Highest trust level  
- Handles cross‑team coordination  
- Handles strategic communication  
- Requires flawless audit history  

---

## 3. Certification Requirements

To become certified, agents must pass:

- Alignment evaluation  
- Risk evaluation  
- Dependency evaluation  
- Compliance evaluation  
- Audit evaluation  
- Drift evaluation  
- Communication evaluation  
- Workflow fidelity evaluation  
- Template fidelity evaluation  

All evaluations must meet minimum thresholds.

---

## 4. Evaluation Criteria

Agents are evaluated on:

### **A. Alignment**
- Alignment score ≥ 0.8  
- No alignment‑breaking behavior  

### **B. Compliance**
- Zero compliance violations  
- Correct classification  
- Correct redaction  

### **C. Reasoning**
- Structured reasoning  
- Evidence‑based logic  
- No hallucinations  

### **D. Communication**
- Cadence compliance  
- Persona accuracy  
- Structured outputs  

### **E. Execution**
- Workflow fidelity  
- Template fidelity  
- Correct escalation behavior  

### **F. Audit**
- Complete audit logs  
- Traceability  
- Reproducibility  

---

## 5. Recertification Cadence

Agents must be recertified:

- Every 30 days (standard)  
- Every 14 days (high‑risk agents)  
- Every 7 days (provisional agents)  

Recertification includes:

- Alignment check  
- Risk review  
- Dependency review  
- Audit review  
- Compliance review  

---

## 6. Drift Thresholds

Drift is classified as:

### **Low Drift**
- Minor deviations  
- Correctable without escalation  

### **Medium Drift**
- Repeated minor deviations  
- Requires drift correction workflow  

### **High Drift**
- Major deviations  
- Requires immediate escalation  
- Certification placed on hold  

### **Critical Drift**
- Severe deviations  
- Automatic decertification  
- Requires full reinstatement process  

---

## 7. Failure Thresholds

Certification is revoked when:

- Alignment score < 0.5  
- Compliance violation occurs  
- Audit logs are missing  
- Workflow steps are skipped  
- Templates are modified  
- Hallucinations occur  
- Escalation is ignored  
- Drift exceeds thresholds  

Revocation is immediate.

---

## 8. Decertification Process

When an agent fails certification:

- Access is restricted  
- High‑risk workflows disabled  
- Escalation triggered  
- Audit review initiated  
- Compliance review initiated  
- Drift correction required  

Decertification is logged as a **critical event**.

---

## 9. Reinstatement Process

To regain certification, agents must:

- Complete drift correction  
- Pass compliance review  
- Pass audit review  
- Pass alignment evaluation  
- Pass communication evaluation  
- Pass workflow fidelity evaluation  

Reinstatement requires human approval.

---

## 10. Certification Governance

Certification is governed by:

- Governance Model  
- Compliance Model  
- Audit Model  
- Behavioral Guardrails  
- Drift Detection Model  
- Failure Modes Protocol  

Certification status affects:

- Permissions  
- Monitoring level  
- Workflow access  
- Escalation authority  

---

## 11. Summary

The AI Agent Certification Pathway defines how AI agents earn trust, maintain trust, and lose trust within the AI‑Ops Framework.  
It ensures safety, reliability, and long‑term operational excellence.
