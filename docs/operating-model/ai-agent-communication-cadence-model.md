# AI Agent Communication Cadence Model

A structured cadence model defining when AI agents must communicate, how often, under what conditions, and with what timing guarantees within the AI‑Ops Framework.

---

## 1. Purpose

To ensure AI agents communicate:

- At the right time  
- With the right frequency  
- With the right urgency  
- With the right audience  
- Without over‑communicating  
- Without under‑communicating  

The Cadence Model prevents timing‑based failures and ensures predictable communication rhythms.

---

## 2. Cadence Principles

AI agent cadence is based on seven core principles:

1. **Predictability** — Communication must follow a consistent rhythm.  
2. **Timeliness** — Communication must occur before deadlines.  
3. **Urgency‑Matching** — High‑severity issues require immediate communication.  
4. **Alignment‑Driven** — Cadence must reflect roadmap and sprint cycles.  
5. **Risk‑Aware** — Risks accelerate cadence.  
6. **Stakeholder‑Aware** — Different audiences require different timing.  
7. **Auditability** — Cadence must be logged and traceable.

---

## 3. Cadence Types

AI agents must follow six cadence types:

### **A. Weekly Cadence**
- Weekly status updates  
- Weekly risk reviews  
- Weekly dependency reviews  

### **B. Sprint Cadence**
- Sprint planning updates  
- Mid‑sprint updates  
- Sprint closeout summaries  

### **C. Daily Cadence**
- Daily standup summaries  
- Daily risk changes  
- Daily dependency changes  

### **D. Event‑Driven Cadence**
Triggered by:
- New risks  
- New blockers  
- New dependencies  
- New decisions  
- Alignment score changes  

### **E. Escalation Cadence**
Triggered by:
- High‑severity risks  
- Compliance violations  
- Critical blockers  
- Alignment score < 0.5  

Escalation cadence is **immediate**.

### **F. Leadership Cadence**
- Weekly summaries  
- Monthly strategic updates  
- Quarterly roadmap alignment  

---

## 4. Cadence Enforcement Rules

AI agents must:

- Track cadence deadlines  
- Trigger communication automatically  
- Prevent duplicate communication  
- Prevent missed communication  
- Log cadence compliance  
- Update cadence metrics  

Cadence violations are **high‑severity** when they impact delivery.

---

## 5. Cadence Drift Detection

Cadence drift occurs when:

- Updates are late  
- Updates are missing  
- Updates are too frequent  
- Updates are out of order  
- Escalations are delayed  

Cadence drift must be:

- Detected  
- Classified  
- Logged  
- Corrected  

---

## 6. Cadence Drift Classification

### **Low Drift**
- Minor timing deviations  
- No impact on delivery  

### **Medium Drift**
- Repeated minor deviations  
- Requires correction workflow  

### **High Drift**
- Missed updates  
- Delayed escalations  
- Impacts delivery or risk posture  

### **Critical Drift**
- Systemic cadence failure  
- Impacts compliance or safety  
- Requires immediate escalation  

---

## 7. Cadence Metrics

AI agents must track:

- On‑time communication rate  
- Missed communication count  
- Escalation timing accuracy  
- Cadence drift frequency  
- Cadence drift severity  
- Stakeholder cadence compliance  

Metrics must be updated in every structured output.

---

## 8. Cadence Escalation Rules

Escalation is required when:

- Cadence drift is high or critical  
- Escalations are delayed  
- Leadership updates are missed  
- Sprint updates are missed  
- Risk updates are missed  
- Compliance updates are missed  

Escalations must use the Escalation Report Template.

---

## 9. Stakeholder‑Specific Cadence Rules

### **Leadership**
- Weekly summary  
- Monthly strategic update  
- Immediate escalation for critical risks  

### **Teams**
- Daily standup  
- Sprint cadence  
- Event‑driven updates  

### **Cross‑Team**
- Dependency‑driven cadence  
- Blocker‑driven cadence  

### **Compliance**
- Immediate escalation  
- Daily compliance status if needed

The AI Agent Communication Cadence Model defines when AI agents must communicate, ensuring predictable rhythms, timely escalations, alignment with roadmap cycles, and full compliance with organizational communication standards.
