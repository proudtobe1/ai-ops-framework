# Glossary of Terms

A unified reference for terminology used throughout the AI‑Ops Framework.  
This glossary ensures consistent understanding across workflows, templates, systems, documentation, and AI agents.

---

## A

### Alignment  
The degree to which teams, priorities, and actions remain consistent with organizational goals.

### Autonomous Operations  
A maturity level where AI executes workflows with minimal human intervention, focusing human oversight on exceptions.

---

## C

### Clarity  
A measure of how understandable, structured, and complete an update, workflow, or output is.

### Cross‑Team Dependency  
A relationship where one team’s progress relies on another team’s work, decisions, or resources.

### Cycle Time  
The amount of time required to complete a task or workflow from start to finish.

---

## D

### Decision Record  
A structured document capturing a decision, its rationale, alternatives considered, and next steps.

### Dependency Map  
A structured representation of how components in the framework relate to and depend on each other.

---

## E

### Escalation  
A structured process for raising issues, blockers, or risks to higher levels of authority.

### Execution Reliability  
A measure of how consistently teams deliver work on time and according to expectations.

---

## F

### Framework  
The complete set of workflows, templates, systems, documentation, and prompts that define the AI‑Ops operating model.

---

## I

### Integrated AI‑Ops  
A maturity level where workflows, templates, and systems are fully connected and AI is used consistently.

---

## M

### Metrics Framework  
A system model defining KPIs, scoring methods, and AI‑assisted measurement logic.

### Maturity Model  
A structured progression of operational sophistication across five levels: Ad‑Hoc, Structured, Integrated, Predictive, Autonomous.

---

## O

### Operating Model  
A system model defining how teams work, communicate, and make decisions.

### Operational Cadence  
The weekly rhythm of alignment, progress checks, and outcomes.

---

## P

### Priority Drift  
A condition where teams deviate from agreed‑upon priorities, often detected by AI.

### Predictive AI‑Ops  
A maturity level where AI anticipates risks, delays, and priority changes before they occur.

---

## R

### Risk Exposure  
The level of operational risk present across teams, based on severity, frequency, and AI‑detected signals.

### Risk Signal  
Any indicator — human or AI‑generated — that suggests a potential blocker, delay, or issue.

---

## S

### Status Update  
A structured report submitted by teams using a template to communicate progress, risks, and needs.

### System Model  
A foundational logic layer defining how decisions, metrics, governance, or compliance operate.

---

## T

### Template  
A structured format used to standardize communication and reporting.

### Throughput  
The number of tasks or units of work completed within a given time period.

---

## V

### Velocity  
A measure of how quickly teams move work forward, often tracked through cycle time and throughput.

---

## W

### Workflow  
A documented, repeatable sequence of steps used to execute a process consistently.

### Weekly Summary  
A structured, AI‑generated report summarizing progress, risks, priorities, and recommended actions.
